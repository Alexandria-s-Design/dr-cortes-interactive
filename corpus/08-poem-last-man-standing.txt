 Obligations of the Last Man Standing

 
 

 by Carlos E. Cortés

 

 

 

 Time passes

 Life moves on

 Then it ends

 At least for others

 But not for me

 Not just yet

 At 92

 

 So when others depart

 Im left to tell their stories

 When their families 

 hold celebrations of life

 When people show up wondering

 How was it way back then

 

 Stories that I remember

 Or try to recall

 Stories that I massage

 To make families happier

 Stories that I invent

 To become part of their legend

 Stories that I hope are true

 Because theyre based on faulty memory

 Because everyones memory is faulty

 

 Thats the role I play

 Thats the role I was destined to play

 Simply because 

 Im the last man standing

 And people turn to me

 To tell them what it was like

 Way back then

 Simply because my memories

 Are still hanging around

 

 I actually remember hearing Pearl Harbor 

 On the radio with my parents

 And the death of Franklin Delano Roosevelt

 And the founding of the United Nations

 And the 1948 presidential election

 of Harry S. Truman

 I actually went to a St. Louis Browns game
 

 and watched Jackie Robinso
n

 play for the Kansas City Monarchs

 

 I swam in racially segregated

 Swimming pools

 And sucked it up 

 When parents told me 

 That I couldnt go out

 With their Protestant daughter

 Because my father was Catholic

 And my mother was Jewish

 

 So I must be an expert

 On their loved ones

 Both the ones I knew well

 And others whom I 

 Talked to occasionally 

 Although maybe not liked

 But happened to be around

 Way back then

 

 So as last man standing

 I continue to tell stories

 At more events

 Hoping they cause 

 their families to smile

 During periods of sadness

 

 My storytelling goes on

 Until its time,

 There will be a time,

 When, I hope,

 People tell stories about me
